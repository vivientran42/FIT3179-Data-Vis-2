Melbourne Airbnb Listings (August 2020)

Since 2008, Airbnb has become an increasingly popular choice for accommodation, whether it be for short-term or long-term travel. The data used in this visualisation includes all Airbnb listings in Melbourne during August 2020. It covers the location of listings, the local government area (LGA) they are situated in, type of accommodation offered, price per night, and other details regarding availability and listing reviews.

Location of Airbnb Listings in Melbourne

These are the locations of all Melbourne Airbnb listings during August 2020. They are coloured by the type of accommodation they offer, for example a private room or entire home. Using the slider at the bottom of the map, the price per night of the listings can also be filtered. Overall, it is evident that many listings are concentrated around the Melbourne CBD area.

Proportion of Accommodation Type per LGA

This visualisation shows the breakdown of type of accommodation in each local government area (LGA). The most common offerings are entire homes/apartments and private rooms. Interestingly, the LGAs have been ordered by descending number of listings. As such, there seems to be a trend the most common accommodation type in LGAs with many listings is entire homes/apartments.

Top 10 LGAs with Highest Number of Listings

These are the local government areas (LGAs) with the most listings. As expected, the City of Melbourne has a significantly higher number of listings than other LGAs. This includes the suburbs of Melbourne CBD, Docklands, South Wharf, South Yarra, and other surrounding neighbourhoods. The LGA with the next highest number of listings is the City of Port Phillip. This includes the suburbs of St Kilda, Windsor, and Elwood.

Top 10 Most Expensive LGAs

This bar chart shows the local government areas (LGAs) with the highest average price per night. The LGA with the highest average price is Knox, with a price of $371.12. This is followed by Bayside with an average price of $266.69.

Maximum Availability of Listings

Hosts can set how many days of the year their listing is available for. For example, if they tend to go on holiday in summer, they can set their home to available for 1 month out of the whole year. This seems to be case with most listings as they are available for 1 to 50 days out of the year. The next most common availability is full availability all year round. These hosts may be utilising their Airbnb listing as a primary source of income and have acquired properties or apartments purely to rent them out.
